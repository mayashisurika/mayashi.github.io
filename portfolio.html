Crafting an Effective CV

Ms. Ishara Raviharee

Curriculum Vitae (CV)

A detailed summary of education, work experience, skills, and achievements.

First Impression Matters

Clear formatting, concise content, and error-free writing are essential.

Tailoring Your CV

Customize it to highlight qualifications relevant to the specific job, and research the company, and include keywords to pass applicant tracking systems (ATS).

Essential Components of a CV

  • Personal Information – Full name, contact details, and optionally LinkedIn or portfolio links.
  • Professional Summary – Briefly highlight career goals and key skills tailored to the job.
  • Work Experience – List roles in reverse chronological order, emphasizing achievements with action verbs and measurable outcomes. There should not be any gaps between working timelines once start to work.
  • Education – Include degrees, majors, institutions, and relevant coursework or honors.
  • Skills – Combine technical and interpersonal skills and be honest about proficiency levels.
  • Achievements and Awards – Showcase awards, recognitions, certifications, or achievements relevant to the role.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Spelling and grammatical errors.
  • Including irrelevant or outdated information.
  • Being overly verbose or too brief.
  • Lack of consistency in formatting.

My Reflection

I learned how to structure my CV effectively, tailor it to specific job roles, and avoid common mistakes which is helpful in my career when applying for jobs. This session highlighted that a well-crafted CV is not just a document but a powerful tool to showcase skills and stand out to employers.

Online/Office Etiquette

24/09/2024

Ms. Ishara Raviharee

Etiquette refers to the rules that dictate appropriate behavior at a certain time and place.

Email Etiquette

  • Always include a subject line and use the recipient’s name in the greeting.
  • Avoid writing in all capital letters, as it can be perceived as shouting.
  • Do not share confidential information via email.
  • Use proper grammar and avoid unnecessary abbreviations or emoticons.
  • Think before sending emails to ensure relevance and appropriateness.
  • Do not forward unnecessary/unrelated emails.

Social Media Use

Be mindful of what you post, as employers and colleges often review applicants. Social media profiles. Maintaining a professional online presence is crucial for personal branding.

Dining etiquette

02/10/2024

Ms. Ishara Raviharee

Throughout this lesson, an understanding of dining etiquette including general behavior, table setting awareness, proper use of utensils, and handling food and drinks, etc. was gained.

  • Start dining only after everyone has been served.
  • Sit upright with good posture, keeping elbows off the table, and maintain polite, composed behavior throughout the meal.
  • Understand the table setup; forks are placed on the left, knives and spoons on the right, with bread plates on the left and glasses above the knives.
  • Rest utensils on your plate when pausing and place them diagonally together to signal you are finished.
  • Avoid speaking with food in your mouth or making noise while eating or drinking.
  • Avoid discussing controversial or inappropriate topics while dining.
  • Thank the hose for the meal and the hospitality, ensure to show appreciation for their effort and company.

My Reflection

I gained insights about proper table manners, using utensils, and dining etiquette fostering confidence and respect in formal and informal dining settings.

Emotional Leakage

Occurs when someone’s inner feelings are expressed more intensely than they like, often in a public setting, and can occur through Voice, Facial Expressions, and Breathing.

Emotional Intelligence

The ability to understand, use, and manage your own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. There are 5 characteristics of Emotional Intelligence,

  • Self-awareness
  • Self-regulation
  • Motivation
  • Empathy
  • Social Skills

Negotiation skills

22/10/2024

Mr. Shenal Rajakarunanayake

It’s the art of reaching an agreement that satisfies both parties. Negotiation is more than communication.

Negotiation types;

  • Professional / Business
  • Personal Relationships
  • Conflict Resolution

To negotiate;

  • Prepare thoroughly – Need to focus on the goal
  • Gather Information – Other parties’ background
  • Identify Interests
  • Set Clear Goals


Negotiation Strategies

  • BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) – Know your alternatives and be prepared to walk away if the deal doesn’t meet the minimum requirements.
  • ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement) – The range where both parties in a negotiation can agree, no plan B.

My Reflection

I gained insights on negotiation skills properly which was a new topic for me including the importance of preparation, active listening, and understanding other party’s interests to create win-win outcomes. Also, we experienced how negotiation is done through role play at the end of the session with our batchmates.
